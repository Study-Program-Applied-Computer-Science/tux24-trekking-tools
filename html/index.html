Advice

Pick the Best Gears

When preparing for a hike, it's crucial to wear proper footwear; invest in a good pair of hiking boots or shoes that provide support and have good traction to ensure comfort and safety on varied terrain. Dressing in layers is equally important; wear moisture-wicking clothing and layer up so you can adjust to changing temperatures and weather conditions. Additionally, always carry a backpack with essential items such as a map, compass or GPS, water, snacks, a first-aid kit, a multi-tool, a flashlight, and extra clothing to be well-prepared for any situation that may arise during your hike

Hiking can be exhausting. That's why it's important to always have good hiking snacks in your backpack. It doesn't matter whether you're going on a short hike or a multi-day hiking tour. Safety and Shelter

Protection from Weather: Weather conditions in mountainous regions can change rapidly. Having a camp provides shelter from rain, wind, and extreme temperatures, ensuring you stay dry and warm.

Nutrition and Energy

A cooking stove allows you to prepare hot meals, which are vital for maintaining energy levels, especially in cold environments where your body burns more calories to stay warm..

Hydration

Dehydration can lead to fatigue, dizziness, confusion, and more severe health issues like heat stroke. Keeping hydrated is essential to maintain your physical performance and overall health

Navigation

Finding Direction: A compass helps you determine your direction relative to magnetic north, which is crucial for navigating through unfamiliar terrain.

What are the health benifits of Trekking ?

Improves uour blood pressure

A study conducted by the CDC recommends that you walk at least an hour a day, five days a week. This reduces the risk of you having a stroke in half! Hiking and trekking regularly improves your heart and blood pressure, minimizing the chance of stroke. Keep that blood pumping!

Better overall fitness

These activities do amazing things to your muscles. Build up strength in your thighs, lower leg muscles, hip muscles and hamstrings. It also builds your endurance. Hiking and trekking are both weight-bearing exercises, which build up bone density. Trekking also boosts your core strength as you hike with a heavy backpack.
